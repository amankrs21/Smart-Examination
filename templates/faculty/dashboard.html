While creating exam, make sure that students know exactly what you want them to do.
Be more explicit about your expectations than you may think is necessary. Otherwise, students may make assumptions that run them into trouble. For example, they may assume, perhaps based on experiences in another course, that an in-class exam is open book or that they can collaborate with classmates on a take-home exam, which you may not allow.
